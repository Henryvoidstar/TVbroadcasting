STUDENT INTERVIEWER:
Today we’re here with Mr. Kapptie, adviser of the Skyline chapter of the Technology Student Association. Thanks for joining us! Can you tell us about the chapter?
ADVISER:
Absolutely. Our Skyline chapter at Skyline High School was founded in 2014, and since then we’ve qualified for national competition almost every single year. That consistency really shows the dedication and talent of our students.
STUDENT INTERVIEWER:
That’s impressive. What kinds of accomplishments have Skyline students achieved?
ADVISER:
We’ve held multiple state-level parliamentary leadership positions, and we’ve also placed — and even won — at nationals in several events. What makes me most proud isn’t just the awards, though. It’s watching students discover skills they didn’t know they had, from engineering design to public speaking.
STUDENT INTERVIEWER:
What are teams preparing for this year?
ADVISER:
This season we have students competing in a huge range of events — robotics, CAD engineering, forensic science, music production, video production, drone challenge, and more. Some events already have full teams, and others are still forming, which means new students can still jump in and be part of something exciting.
STUDENT INTERVIEWER:
Why should students consider joining TSA?
ADVISER:
TSA is where creativity meets real-world problem solving. You get to build, design, present, collaborate, and compete — all while preparing for college and careers. It’s one of the best places for students to discover what they’re capable of.
